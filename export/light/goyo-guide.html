How it works

Tap to travel

Your journal is fully hyperlinked. Nothing to scroll through — tap, and you are there. Use a reader that follows internal links (GoodNotes or Notability).

  • Month railThe vertical JAN–DEC strip on the right edge jumps to any month, from any page.
  • Year pageTap any month to open its calendar.
  • Monthly calendarTap any date to open that day’s page.
  • Daily pageTap the large date number (or the month name) to return to the month.
  • Monthly notesOn the year page, tap a month’s “notes” chip to open its notes page.
  • Top tabsYear · Habits · Notes · Gratitude. Habits and Notes open the current month’s pages; Gratitude is the last page.
Tip: in GoodNotes, make sure you are in reading / hand mode (not the pen) so a tap follows the link instead of drawing.
Get set up

Importing your journal

After purchase the PDF is available to download right away. Save it somewhere easy to find, then add it to your note-taking app.

GoodNotes (iPad / iPhone)
  1. Tap the downloaded file to open it.
  2. Tap the Share icon and choose Open in GoodNotes (or the GoodNotes icon).
  3. When GoodNotes opens, a window pops up.
  4. Rename the file if you like, and pick a folder.
  5. Choose Import as New Document and tap Import.
Notability

Share → Open in Notability, or import from the Notability library. Internal links work the same way.

Undated by design — no year, no weekdays. Write the dates that suit you, and reuse the journal every year.
Make it yours

Reuse & rearrange

Duplicate a page (e.g. extra daily or notes)
  1. Open the page you want to copy.
  2. Tap the ••• (menu) in the upper-right corner.
  3. Choose Copy, then add a new page where you want it and Paste Page.
Move a page
  1. Tap the four-square (thumbnail) icon, top-left.
  2. Drag the page to its new spot in the document.
Keep your links working. Move and duplicate freely — links travel with the pages. Just don’t delete the original section/template pages, or some links will have nowhere to land. Copied pages keep their contents; clear them with the eraser or lasso tool to start fresh.
